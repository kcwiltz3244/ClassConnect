WHY I CREATED CLASSCONNECT

Our 50th reunion brought us together again and reminded me that time waits for no one.

In 1976, we crossed the stage together with diplomas in our hands and dreams in our hearts. What felt like the end of our school years was really the beginning of a new chapter. None of us could have imagined where life would lead over the next fifty years.

We each set out on our own journey. We built careers, raised families, served our communities, celebrated milestones, overcame challenges, and created lives uniquely our own. Although our stories unfolded in different places, they all began with the friendships we formed as members of the St. Martinville Senior High School Class of 1976.

At our reunion, we laughed, exchanged stories, remembered old times, and reconnected with friends we had not seen in years. We also felt the absence of classmates we had lost along the way. I realized that some classmates had faced illness, hardship, and major changes in their lives without many of us ever knowing. We never had a single platform where we could stay connected.

At the end of the reunion, we hugged, said goodbye, and made the familiar promise:

“Let’s keep in touch.”

I created ClassConnect because I did not want that promise to fade once everyone returned to their everyday lives. ClassConnect is more than a directory or a reunion website. It is our living yearbook—a place where we can share our stories, celebrate milestones, support one another, remember the classmates we have lost, renew old friendships, and continue writing new chapters together.

Welcome home. Our journey continues.

— Kevin Wiltz

Our Journey

More than a reunion. A lifetime of friendships.

1964
🏫

WHERE IT ALL BEGAN

Notre Dame Catholic School

Some of us first met in 1964 at Notre Dame Catholic School. We still remember the sound of Mother Bridget ringing the school bell—and, yes, the leather strap that reminded us to stay on our best behavior. Those memories still bring smiles and laughter more than sixty years later.

Back then, Catholic schools were still segregated. None of us realized we were living through a chapter of history that would shape both our community and our lives.

1971–72
🤝

A NEW BEGINNING

Our Paths Came Together

In 1971, many of us became part of the first year of integration at Trinity Catholic Elementary School. Then, in 1972, as the Catholic schools ended with the eighth grade, many of us entered St. Martinville Junior High together.

Something special happened there. Classmates became teammates. Neighbors became lifelong friends. We laughed together, learned together, got into a little trouble together, and grew up together. That was where the journey of the St. Martinville Senior High School Class of 1976 truly began.

1976
🎓

THE ROAD AHEAD

Life Took Us in Different Directions

Graduation came in 1976. Like seeds carried by the wind, our lives spread in many different directions. Some stayed close to home. Others crossed the country.

We built careers, raised families, served our communities, celebrated milestones, faced hardships, and created lives we could never have imagined as teenagers. Some friendships stayed strong. Others quietly faded—not because we stopped caring, but simply because life happened.

10–50
⏳

THE SPEED OF TIME

Where Did the Years Go?

When we were teenagers, fifty years seemed like forever. Then one reunion became another: ten years, fifteen years, twenty years—and somehow we found ourselves celebrating our 50th reunion.

Time never asked our permission. It simply kept moving. Every reunion reminded us that the years disappear far more quickly than we ever imagined.

Always
🌳

THE FRIENDS WE CARRY WITH US

Forever Part of Our Class

Along the way, we lost classmates we loved. Every reunion includes familiar faces—and faces we wish were still sitting beside us. They may no longer answer roll call, but they will always answer in our hearts.

Our Legacy Garden exists because memories deserve a home. Every photograph, every shared story, and every laugh remembered keeps their spirit alive within our class family.

Today
💜

WHY WE BUILT CLASS CONNECT

Friendships Deserve More Than a Reunion

This community was created so birthdays do not go unnoticed, accomplishments can be celebrated, prayers and encouragement can be shared, and classmates can reconnect before another decade slips away.

Because one phone call, one message, one visit, or one sympathetic ear can mean more than we may ever know.

Next
✨

OUR PROMISE

Our Journey Isn’t Over

Let’s not wait for another reunion to reconnect. Let’s not learn months later that someone was sick. Let’s not say, “I wish I had called,” “I wish I had reached out,” or “I wish I had known.”

Instead, let’s celebrate each other’s victories, lift one another through difficult days, share our stories, laugh like classmates again, and remember those who walked beside us.

Because although our school days are behind us, our journey isn’t over.

Let’s Class Connect.
